## Overview

Responsible AI integration in anesthesiology requires more than individual clinician awareness — it requires institutional governance. These principles provide a framework for evaluating, deploying, and monitoring AI tools within your department or health system.

<Callout kind="info">
  These governance principles complement the [VDD Framework](/ai/vdd-framework). VDD guides individual clinical decisions; governance structures guide institutional AI policy.
</Callout>

## Four Pillars of AI Governance

<Columns cols={2}>
  <Card title="Ethical Oversight" icon="shield">
    Establish review boards or committees responsible for evaluating AI tools before clinical deployment. Review should assess clinical validity, bias potential, data privacy, and alignment with institutional values.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Transparency" icon="eye">
    Require disclosure of AI assistance in all clinical documentation. Clinicians, patients, and quality review teams should be able to identify when and how AI influenced clinical decisions.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Continuous Audit" icon="trending-up">
    Monitor AI tool performance through regular outcome reviews. Track concordance between AI recommendations and clinical decisions, and measure patient outcomes associated with AI-influenced care.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Training" icon="book-open">
    Train all clinical staff on the VDD framework, AI limitations, and institutional AI policies. New hires should complete AI governance training before using AI-assisted tools in clinical settings.
  </Card>
</Columns>

## Building an AI Review Board

An effective AI review board evaluates AI tools at three stages: pre-deployment, active use, and retirement.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pre-Deployment Review" icon="search">
    Before any AI tool enters clinical use, the review board should assess:

    - **Clinical evidence** — Published validation studies in relevant patient populations
    - **Bias assessment** — Performance across demographic groups and clinical scenarios
    - **Data governance** — How patient data is collected, stored, processed, and shared
    - **Integration impact** — How the tool fits into existing clinical workflows
    - **Failure modes** — What happens when the AI produces incorrect or unavailable outputs
  </Step>
  <Step title="Active Monitoring" icon="activity">
    During clinical use, the board should oversee:

    - Regular performance metrics against established benchmarks
    - Incident reporting for AI-related clinical events
    - Clinician feedback on usability, accuracy, and workflow impact
    - Compliance with documentation and VDD requirements
  </Step>
  <Step title="Retirement Criteria" icon="alert-triangle">
    Define clear criteria for removing an AI tool from clinical use:

    - Performance falls below established accuracy thresholds
    - Systematic bias is identified that cannot be corrected
    - The vendor can no longer guarantee data governance standards
    - Superior alternatives become available with better evidence
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Transparency in Clinical Documentation

When AI tools inform clinical decisions, documentation should include:

| Element | Example |
|---------|---------|
| AI tool identified | "AI-assisted dosage calculator (v2.1) consulted" |
| Recommendation stated | "Suggested propofol induction dose: 2 mg/kg" |
| Verification performed | "Verified against ASA guidelines and patient BMI/age" |
| Clinical decision | "Dose adjusted to 1.5 mg/kg due to patient age (78 years)" |
| Rationale documented | "Reduced dose per geriatric dosing recommendations" |

## Audit and Quality Improvement

Establish a regular audit cycle to evaluate AI tool performance:

- **Monthly:** Review incident reports and clinician feedback
- **Quarterly:** Analyze concordance rates (how often clinicians agree with AI recommendations)
- **Annually:** Comprehensive performance review against clinical outcomes data
- **Ad hoc:** Triggered by sentinel events or significant software updates

<Callout kind="warning">
  AI tool updates and version changes can alter recommendation patterns. Re-evaluate performance after every significant software update from the vendor.
</Callout>

## Training Requirements

All clinicians using AI-assisted tools should complete:

1. **VDD Framework training** — Understanding the Verify-Decide-Document methodology ([VDD Framework guide](/ai/vdd-framework))
2. **Tool-specific orientation** — Hands-on training with each AI tool used in clinical practice
3. **Documentation standards** — How to record AI use in clinical notes per institutional policy
4. **Annual refresher** — Updated training incorporating lessons learned from audits and incidents
