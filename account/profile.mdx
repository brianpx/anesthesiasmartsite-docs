## Overview

Your profile settings control how the platform personalizes content recommendations, how you receive notifications, and how the interface appears.

## Professional Details

Update your professional information to improve content recommendations:

- **Name** — Your display name across the platform
- **Specialty focus** — Select your primary specialty (e.g., neuroanesthesia, cardiac, obstetric, pediatric) to prioritize relevant protocols and education
- **Medical license number** — Optional; used for CME credit documentation
- **Institution** — Your hospital or practice name for institutional content filtering

Access these settings from **Profile** in the account menu.

## Notification Preferences

Configure how you receive updates about new content, CME opportunities, and platform changes:

| Notification Type | Options |
|-------------------|---------|
| New CME content | Email, in-app, or both |
| Protocol updates | Email, in-app, or both |
| Changelog updates | Email or off |
| Community activity | Email digest (daily/weekly) or off |

## Display Preferences

### Dark Mode

Toggle dark mode for low-light environments common during night shifts and on-call coverage.

- **Manual toggle:** Access from the display toggle in the top navigation bar
- **System preference:** Set to "Auto" to follow your device's system-wide dark/light mode setting

For detailed setup instructions, see [Use Dark Mode](/help-center/guides/dark-mode).

### Content Density

Choose between standard and compact content layouts for protocol and reference pages based on your reading preference.

## Email Preferences

Manage which emails you receive from the platform:

- **Newsletter** — Regular updates on new content and features
- **CME reminders** — Notifications about incomplete CME activities and upcoming expiration dates
- **Platform announcements** — Major feature releases and policy changes

Unsubscribe from specific email types from the **Email Preferences** section of your profile or via the unsubscribe link in any email.
