## Overview

This page covers the anesthetic techniques used for awake spine surgery and other MAC procedures. The standard approach uses dexmedetomidine-based sedation combined with local anesthesia, targeting cooperative sedation that preserves the patient's ability to communicate during neurological assessments.

<Callout kind="warning">
  Drug dosages and protocols must be adapted to your institutional formulary, patient-specific factors, and clinical circumstances. Always verify dosing against current references and patient parameters.
</Callout>

## Dexmedetomidine Protocol

Dexmedetomidine is the preferred sedation agent for awake spine procedures due to its analgesic and anxiolytic properties without significant respiratory depression.

### Loading Dose

| Parameter | Standard Protocol | Adjusted Protocol |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Dose | 1 mcg/kg IV | 0.5 mcg/kg IV |
| Duration | Over 10 minutes | Over 10-15 minutes |
| Indication | Standard patients | Elderly, baseline bradycardia, cardiac disease |

### Maintenance Infusion

| Parameter | Range |
|-----------|-------|
| Initial rate | 0.2-0.7 mcg/kg/hr |
| Titration target | Ramsay Sedation Score 2-3 |
| Adjustment interval | Every 5-10 minutes based on clinical response |
| Neurological assessment | Reduce or pause infusion to achieve Ramsay 2 |

<Callout kind="tip">
  Start the maintenance infusion at the lower end of the dosing range and titrate up. Patients vary significantly in their response to dexmedetomidine — conservative initial dosing reduces the risk of excessive sedation and hemodynamic effects.
</Callout>

### Hemodynamic Monitoring

Dexmedetomidine commonly causes bradycardia and hypotension. Prepare for these effects:

- **Bradycardia:** Glycopyrrolate 0.2-0.4 mg IV at the bedside. Consider reducing loading dose in patients with resting heart rate below 60 bpm.
- **Hypotension:** Phenylephrine 50-100 mcg IV bolus or ephedrine 5-10 mg IV. Ensure adequate IV fluid status before starting the infusion.
- **Monitoring frequency:** Blood pressure every 3-5 minutes during loading, every 5 minutes during maintenance.

## Sedation Scoring

Use the Ramsay Sedation Scale to standardize sedation assessment and documentation.

| Score | Level | Clinical Description |
|-------|-------|---------------------|
| 1 | Anxious, agitated | Patient anxious, agitated, or restless |
| 2 | Cooperative, calm | Patient calm, cooperative, oriented — **target for neurological assessment** |
| 3 | Drowsy, responds to commands | Patient drowsy but responds to verbal commands — **acceptable maintenance level** |
| 4 | Asleep, brisk response | Asleep, brisk response to light glabellar tap or loud auditory stimulus |
| 5 | Asleep, sluggish response | Asleep, sluggish response to stimuli |
| 6 | Unresponsive | No response to stimuli — **requires intervention** |

<Callout kind="info">
  Target Ramsay 2-3 for the procedural phase. For neurological assessments (motor/sensory testing), aim for Ramsay 2 — fully cooperative and oriented. Document sedation scores at minimum every 15 minutes.
</Callout>

## Local Anesthesia Protocols

### Skin and Subcutaneous Infiltration

| Agent | Concentration | Additives | Use |
|-------|---------------|-----------|-----|
| Lidocaine | 1% | Epinephrine 1:200,000 | Skin infiltration, rapid onset |
| Bupivacaine | 0.25% | With or without epinephrine | Deeper tissue infiltration, prolonged analgesia |

### Maximum Dose Guidelines

| Agent | Without Epinephrine | With Epinephrine |
|-------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Lidocaine | 4.5 mg/kg | 7 mg/kg |
| Bupivacaine | 2.5 mg/kg | 3 mg/kg |

<Callout kind="warning">
  Calculate maximum doses based on actual patient weight. Track total local anesthetic volume administered throughout the procedure to avoid toxicity. Have lipid emulsion (Intralipid 20%) immediately available for local anesthetic systemic toxicity (LAST).
</Callout>

### Local Anesthetic Systemic Toxicity (LAST)

Recognize early signs and have rescue protocols available:

- **CNS symptoms:** Perioral numbness, metallic taste, tinnitus, visual disturbances, agitation
- **Cardiovascular:** Hypotension, bradycardia, arrhythmias
- **Treatment:** Stop injection, secure airway, IV lipid emulsion per ASRA guidelines

## Supplemental Agents

| Agent | Dose | Indication |
|-------|------|------------|
| Midazolam | 0.5-1 mg IV | Pre-operative anxiolysis (use sparingly — may impair neurological assessment) |
| Fentanyl | 25-50 mcg IV | Supplemental analgesia for positioning or surgical stimulation |
| Ketamine | 0.1-0.3 mg/kg IV | Low-dose adjunct for analgesic augmentation |

<Callout kind="info">
  Use supplemental agents judiciously. Oversedation impairs the patient's ability to participate in neurological assessments, negating the primary advantage of the awake technique.
</Callout>
