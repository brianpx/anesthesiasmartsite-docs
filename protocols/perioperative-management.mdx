## Overview

This page provides general perioperative management protocols applicable across anesthesia practice. These checklists and criteria serve as structured references for standard workflow phases — pre-operative assessment, induction, maintenance, emergence, and post-operative care.

<Callout kind="warning">
  Protocols must be adapted to your institutional policies, patient population, and individual clinical circumstances. This content does not replace clinical judgment.
</Callout>

## Pre-Operative Assessment Checklist

<Steps>
  <Step title="Patient Identification and Consent" icon="user">
    - Verify patient identity using two identifiers
    - Confirm surgical procedure, site, and laterality
    - Review informed consent for anesthesia
    - Confirm NPO status per institutional guidelines
  </Step>
  <Step title="Medical History Review" icon="clipboard-list">
    - Review medical conditions, surgical history, and anesthetic history
    - Document current medications, allergies, and adverse reactions
    - Assess for personal or family history of anesthetic complications (malignant hyperthermia, prolonged paralysis, PONV)
    - Review recent laboratory results, imaging, and consultations
  </Step>
  <Step title="Airway Assessment" icon="wind">
    - Mallampati classification
    - Thyromental distance and neck mobility
    - Mouth opening and dental assessment
    - History of prior difficult intubation
    - BMI and habitus relevant to airway management
  </Step>
  <Step title="Equipment and Plan" icon="settings">
    - Confirm anesthesia machine check completed
    - Medications drawn and labeled per institutional policy
    - Monitoring equipment ready (standard ASA monitors minimum)
    - Airway equipment selected and backup devices available
    - Blood products ordered and available if anticipated need
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Induction Checklist

Complete before induction of general anesthesia:

| Item | Status |
|------|--------|
| Time-out completed (procedure, site, patient confirmed) | Verify |
| IV access established and functional | Verify |
| Standard ASA monitors applied and functional | Verify |
| Pre-oxygenation completed (3-5 minutes or 8 vital capacity breaths) | Verify |
| Induction agents, neuromuscular blocker, and emergency medications drawn | Verify |
| Suction available and tested | Verify |
| Airway equipment ready (primary and backup devices) | Verify |
| Ventilator settings pre-set | Verify |

## Intra-Operative Maintenance

### Monitoring Standards

- **Standard ASA monitors:** SpO2, ECG, NIBP, capnography, temperature
- **Additional monitoring as indicated:** Invasive arterial pressure, central venous pressure, neuromuscular monitoring, BIS/processed EEG
- **Documentation frequency:** Vital signs recorded at minimum every 5 minutes

### Fluid Management

| Maintenance | Guideline |
|-------------|-----------|
| Baseline rate | 4-2-1 rule for hourly maintenance (first 10 kg: 4 mL/kg/hr, next 10 kg: 2 mL/kg/hr, remaining: 1 mL/kg/hr) |
| NPO deficit replacement | Replace deficit over first 1-2 hours |
| Surgical losses | Replace based on surgical field assessment and estimated blood loss |
| Urine output target | 0.5-1 mL/kg/hr |

### Hemodynamic Goals

Maintain hemodynamic parameters within 20% of patient's baseline unless specific surgical requirements dictate otherwise.

## Emergence Criteria

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pre-Emergence Assessment" icon="check-circle">
    Before initiating emergence, confirm:

    - Surgical procedure is complete and wound is closed or being closed
    - Neuromuscular blockade is adequately reversed (train-of-four ratio greater than 0.9)
    - Patient temperature is within acceptable range (above 36 degrees Celsius)
    - Pain management plan is initiated (multimodal analgesia preferred)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Emergence Process" icon="sunrise">
    - Reduce or discontinue maintenance anesthetics
    - Resume spontaneous ventilation
    - Suction oropharynx as needed
    - Extubate when protective reflexes have returned and patient follows commands
    - Apply supplemental oxygen
  </Step>
  <Step title="Post-Emergence Monitoring" icon="activity">
    - Continuous SpO2 and ECG during transport to PACU
    - Assess level of consciousness and airway patency
    - Administer supplemental oxygen as needed
    - Monitor for complications (laryngospasm, bronchospasm, PONV, emergence delirium)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Post-Operative Care

### PACU Assessment

| Assessment | Frequency | Notes |
|------------|-----------|-------|
| Vital signs | Every 5 minutes for first 15 minutes, then every 15 minutes | SpO2, BP, HR, RR, temperature |
| Pain assessment | On arrival, then with each vital sign set | Use validated pain scale |
| Level of consciousness | On arrival, then with each vital sign set | Aldrete score or institutional equivalent |
| Nausea/vomiting | On arrival, then as needed | Treat per institutional PONV protocol |
| Surgical site | On arrival, then hourly | Check dressing, drainage, neurovascular status |

### Discharge Criteria

Standard PACU discharge criteria (Aldrete Score or institutional equivalent):

- Activity: able to move all extremities voluntarily or on command
- Respiration: able to breathe deeply and cough freely
- Circulation: blood pressure within 20% of pre-operative baseline
- Consciousness: fully awake
- SpO2: maintains above 92% on room air (or at patient's baseline)
- Pain: adequately managed
- PONV: controlled

<Callout kind="tip">
  Document all deviations from protocol and the clinical rationale for those decisions. This supports quality assurance reviews and continuous improvement of institutional protocols.
</Callout>
