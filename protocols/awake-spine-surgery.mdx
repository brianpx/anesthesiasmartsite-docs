## Overview

Awake spine surgery under monitored anesthesia care (MAC) allows real-time neurological monitoring and patient feedback during spinal procedures. This protocol covers the complete procedural workflow from pre-operative evaluation through post-operative care.

<Callout kind="warning">
  This protocol must be adapted to your institutional policies, patient population, and individual clinical circumstances. This content does not replace clinical judgment.
</Callout>

## Indications

Awake spine surgery is indicated for procedures where real-time neurological assessment provides a safety advantage:

- Lumbar decompression procedures
- Certain spinal fusion procedures
- Cases where intra-operative neurological monitoring via patient feedback improves outcomes
- Patients with comorbidities that increase general anesthesia risk

## Pre-Operative Phase

<Steps>
  <Step title="Patient Selection" icon="users">
    Confirm the patient meets the [selection criteria](/protocols/patient-selection):

    - ASA physical status I-II (consider ASA III on case-by-case basis)
    - Psychological suitability for awake procedure (anxiety screening completed)
    - BMI within institutional guidelines (typically less than 30 kg/m2)
    - No contraindications to MAC or sedation agents

    See [Patient Selection Criteria](/protocols/patient-selection) for the complete assessment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pre-Operative Counseling" icon="message-circle">
    Discuss the following with the patient:

    - What to expect during an awake procedure (sounds, sensations, positioning)
    - How communication will work during the case
    - The option to convert to general anesthesia if needed
    - Expected duration and recovery timeline
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pre-Operative Assessment" icon="clipboard-list">
    Complete standard pre-anesthetic evaluation with additional focus on:

    - Airway assessment (relevant for potential conversion to general anesthesia)
    - Anxiety level and coping strategies
    - Current medications that may interact with sedation agents
    - Baseline neurological examination and documentation
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Intra-Operative Phase

### Anesthetic Technique

The standard approach uses MAC with dexmedetomidine infusion. See [Anesthetic Techniques](/protocols/anesthetic-techniques) for detailed drug protocols.

**Setup checklist:**
- Standard ASA monitors applied
- IV access established (two lines recommended)
- Dexmedetomidine infusion prepared
- Emergency airway equipment immediately available
- Rescue medications drawn up (glycopyrrolate, phenylephrine, propofol)

### Sedation Management

| Phase | Target Sedation | Agent | Notes |
|-------|-----------------|-------|-------|
| Positioning | Ramsay 2 (cooperative, calm) | Dexmedetomidine loading | Patient assists with positioning |
| Surgical prep | Ramsay 2-3 | Dexmedetomidine infusion | Local anesthesia infiltration |
| Procedure | Ramsay 2-3 | Dexmedetomidine maintenance | Maintain verbal contact |
| Neurological checks | Ramsay 2 (alert) | Reduce/pause infusion | Full patient cooperation needed |
| Closure | Ramsay 2-3 | Resume maintenance rate | Pain management transition |

### Local Anesthesia

- **Skin and subcutaneous:** Lidocaine 1% with epinephrine 1:200,000
- **Deeper tissues:** Bupivacaine 0.25% for longer-acting analgesia
- **Maximum doses:** Observe institutional maximum dose guidelines accounting for patient weight
- **Timing:** Allow adequate onset time before surgical incision

### Monitoring During Procedure

- Continuous verbal communication with the patient
- Standard ASA monitors (SpO2, ECG, NIBP, EtCO2 via nasal cannula)
- Sedation scoring at regular intervals (every 15 minutes minimum)
- Hemodynamic monitoring with attention to dexmedetomidine-associated bradycardia

<Callout kind="tip">
  Establish a clear communication protocol with the patient before the procedure begins. Agree on simple commands and response methods (e.g., squeeze hand, wiggle toes) for neurological assessments.
</Callout>

## Post-Operative Phase

<Steps>
  <Step title="Emergence and Recovery" icon="sunrise">
    - Discontinue dexmedetomidine infusion per institutional protocol
    - Monitor for rebound hypertension or tachycardia
    - Assess neurological status and compare to pre-operative baseline
    - Transfer to PACU with standard handoff
  </Step>
  <Step title="PACU Management" icon="heart">
    - Continue standard post-operative monitoring
    - Pain management transition to multimodal analgesia
    - Repeat neurological assessment and document
    - Monitor for delayed complications (bleeding, neurological changes)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Documentation" icon="file-text">
    Record in the anesthetic chart:
    - Sedation agents, doses, and infusion rates
    - Sedation scores throughout the procedure
    - Local anesthetic agents, concentrations, and volumes
    - All neurological assessments performed and results
    - Any complications or deviations from protocol
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Conversion to General Anesthesia

Have a clear plan for converting to general anesthesia if needed:

- **Indications for conversion:** Uncontrolled patient anxiety, inadequate analgesia despite maximum local anesthesia, hemodynamic instability, surgical complication requiring deeper sedation
- **Preparation:** Airway equipment checked and immediately available at case start
- **Communication:** Inform the surgical team immediately when conversion is being considered
