## What is the VDD Framework?

VDD stands for **Verify, Decide, Document** — a three-step methodology for supervised AI use in clinical settings. You verify AI recommendations against evidence, decide on the appropriate clinical action, and document the process. See the full [VDD Framework](/ai/vdd-framework) guide.

## Is AI making clinical decisions on this platform?

No. AI tools on Anesthesia SmartSite provide recommendations and information only. All clinical decisions remain with the treating clinician. The VDD framework ensures that AI outputs are verified and documented rather than blindly followed.

## How are AI recommendations validated?

AI recommendations referenced on the platform are evaluated against published clinical guidelines, society recommendations, and peer-reviewed evidence. The [AI Governance Principles](/ai/governance) page describes the review and audit processes in detail.

## What should I do if an AI recommendation seems incorrect?

Apply the VDD framework:
1. **Verify** — Check the recommendation against your institutional protocols and patient data
2. **Decide** — If the recommendation is inconsistent with evidence or clinical judgment, reject or modify it
3. **Document** — Record that the AI recommendation was received, your verification steps, and your clinical rationale

Report potentially inaccurate AI content to [support@anesthesiasmartsite.com](mailto:support@anesthesiasmartsite.com).

## Does the platform store patient data?

The educational platform itself does not require or store patient data. If your institution integrates AI tools referenced on the platform, data governance is managed by your institution's policies. See [Privacy & Data Security](/help-center/faq/privacy-security) for more details.
