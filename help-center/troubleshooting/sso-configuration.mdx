## SSO Login Not Working

If you cannot log in via your institution's SSO:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Verify SSO Is Configured" icon="check-circle">
    Confirm with your IT department that Anesthesia SmartSite SSO integration has been set up for your institution. SSO is not enabled by default — your institution must configure it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use the Correct Login Method" icon="shield">
    On the login page, select **Login with SSO** and choose your institution's identity provider. Do not enter your institutional credentials in the standard email/password fields.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Clear Browser Data" icon="trash">
    Clear cookies and cached data for both the Anesthesia SmartSite domain and your identity provider domain. Stale session cookies can cause authentication loops.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check Network Requirements" icon="wifi">
    Some SSO providers require you to be on the institutional network or VPN. Try connecting through your institution's network if you are working remotely.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Supported SSO Providers

Anesthesia SmartSite supports SSO through:

| Provider Type | Examples |
|---------------|----------|
| Google Workspace | Institutional Google accounts |
| SAML 2.0 | Hospital identity providers, Okta, Azure AD, OneLogin |

## For IT Administrators

If you are setting up SSO for your institution:

1. Contact [support@anesthesiasmartsite.com](mailto:support@anesthesiasmartsite.com) with the subject line "SSO Configuration Request"
2. Include your institution name and preferred identity provider
3. The Anesthesia SmartSite team will provide the SAML metadata and configuration endpoints
4. After configuration, test with a pilot group before rolling out to all users

<Callout kind="info">
  SSO configuration requires coordination between your IT department and the Anesthesia SmartSite team. Allow 1-2 business days for initial setup and testing.
</Callout>

## Still Having Issues?

Contact [support@anesthesiasmartsite.com](mailto:support@anesthesiasmartsite.com) with:
- Your institution name
- The identity provider being used
- The error message displayed during login
- Whether the issue affects all users or specific accounts
